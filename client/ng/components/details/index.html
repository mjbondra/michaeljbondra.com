JavaScript All the Things

Let's talk about JavaScript. It's not perfect — an obvious statement for those who have lived through the nightmares of loaded operators, unintuitive coercion, and 0.1 + 0.2 !== 0.3. But a server-side runtime, evolving standards, and a myriad of excellent frameworks have given new life to the imperfect child of necessity.

I can help you with your next Express app, but I really want you to hear more about Koa. I want you to see the power of ECMAScript 6 in practice — in generators that allow you to build synchronicity within asynchronous code through yield statements. The ability to flatten callback pyramids and decrease your reliance on Promises is a liberation that every JavaScript developer should experience.

On the front-end, let's choose libraries that efficiently serve your purpose — let's choose a framework that allows us to write code that is clean, modular, and scalable. AngularJS — sponsored by Google and supported by a huge community — is a dependency-injecting, two-way-data-binding wunderkind that is worthy of consideration for a variety of projects. I pair it with Browserify, which allows me to easily share code and methodology with Node-based server components.
